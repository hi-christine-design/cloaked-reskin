What's changing visually

Current (iOS Design System 4.0) Reskin (cloaked-design-md)
Native iOS look (SF Pro, light cream backgrounds, subtle shadows) Editorial: dark / light / dark section layering, Simula serif headlines
High information density per screen Restrained density — minimal competing elements
Generic — hard to distinguish from other iOS apps Recognizable Cloaked visual language at a glance

Why this matters

Trust is the product. Cloaked sells privacy and identity protection. In this category, how the app feels is itself part of the value — users need to take us seriously. Serif headlines, careful typography, and editorial rhythm signal craftsmanship and trustworthiness in a way standard iOS chrome can't.

Brand differentiation. The existing design is well-made but visually indistinguishable from dozens of other iOS apps. The reskin is recognizably Cloaked at a glance — important when competitors (Aura, DeleteMe, etc.) all look similar.

Premium positioning. Privacy is a premium subscription service. The reskin's visual language supports a higher price point and reduces price resistance. A generic iOS look unconsciously anchors users to commodity perception.

Marketing / product consistency. The reskin aligns with Cloaked's marketing site, ads, and brand materials. Right now there's a visual disconnect — marketing looks one way, the app looks another.

Better information hierarchy. Dark hero → light dashboard → dark FAQ creates clear scan zones. Fewer competing UI elements per screen means the important things (digital risk score, actions taken, FAQ) get the attention they deserve.
